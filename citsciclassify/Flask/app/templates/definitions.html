Category Definitions

These are the descriptions for each of the Topic Categories for Outbreak.info. There are 9 broad categories, and 24 specific subcategories.

Whenever possible, please select the most appropriate subcategories in lieu of the broad category.

If you find that a specific subcategory is appropriate, there is NO need to select the corresponding broad category.

  • Clinical - Clinical presentations of COVID-19. This encompasses work related to the observation and treatment of actual patients.
    • Case Descriptions - Descriptions of specific patient cases. e.g.- Clinical Case Report.
    • Risk Factors - Risk factors associated with case presentation, severity, or mortality
  • Diagnosis - Disease assessment through symptoms, test results, and radiological features
    • Symptoms - Diagnosis based off clinical symptoms
    • Rapid Diagnostics - Diagnosis based off of rapid testing procedures and generally enable point of care testing.
    • Antibody Detection - Antibody-based detection. Enables determination of past history of infection by looking at an aspect of the body's immunological response to the virus.
    • Virus Detection - RT-PCR detection. Enables determination of active infection by looking for the presence of viral genetic material.
    • Testing Prevalence - Extent of testing
    • Pathology/Radiology - Diagnosis based off pathology and/or radiology. This often involves some sort of patient sampling (e.g.- like a biopsy or dissection), and some sort of imaging technology (e.g.- microscopy, x-rays, ct scans, etc.)
  • Forecasting - Modelling and estimating the trend of COVID-19 spread
  • Mechanism - Underlying cause(s) of COVID-19 infections and transmission and possible drug mechanism of action
    • Virus Factors - How virus factors influence infection and transmission. This includes viral genes and proteins.
    • Host Factors - How host genetic factors influence infection and transmission. This includes variant genes and proteins in the host.
    • Immunological Response - Description of immunological response upon virus exposure.
    • Mechanism of Infection - Description of how the virus enters and infects human cells
    • Mechanism of Transmission - Description of how the virus transmits between hosts
  • Prevention - Prevention, control, and management strategies either at the individual or societal level
    • Public Health Interventions - Descriptions of public health interventions and/or evaluations of their efficacy
    • Individual Prevention - Descriptions of individual transmission prevention methods and/or their efficacy, including behavior change campaigns
  • Transmission - Characteristics and modes of COVID-19 transmissions, such as human-to-human
    • Host/Intermediate Reservoirs - Descriptions of the host source of SARS-CoV-2 and its spread through intermediate reservoirs
    • Viral Shedding / Persistence - How much the virus sheds from host and how long it persists in environments
  • Treatment - Treatment strategies, therapeutic procedures, and vaccine development
    • Vaccines - Vaccine development and immune response
    • Pharmaceutical Treatments - Development of new pharmaceutical treatments of COVID-19, especially small molecule development
    • Repurposing - Repurposing candidates of approved drugs
    • Biologics - Development of biologically based treatments of COVID-19, including the use of convalescent plasma
    • Medical Care - Medical interventions or procedures to treat COVID-19 such as dialysis, ventilators, etc.
  • Epidemiology - Description of the spread of cases
    • Molecular epidemiology - Description of chains of transmission, R0, doubling time, dating outbreak in specific regions/overall based on molecular/genetic assays
    • Classical epidemiology - Description of cases, chains of transmission, broader scale dynamics (R0, doubling time, duration of infectiousness,...) based on classical epidemiological methods like case counts and contact tracing
  • Behavioral Research - Mental health, behavioral, or social science research directly or indirectly related to COVID-19 such as studies on depression or social isolation, incidence of domestic violence, protests against public health directives, and more.
